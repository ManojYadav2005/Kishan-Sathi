⚖️ Organic vs Chemical Farming

Data-driven comparison to help you choose the right approach for your fields

🌱 Organic Farming

  • Uses compost, green manure, vermicompost
  • Natural pest control (neem, bio-agents)
  • Builds long-term soil health
  • Lower input costs after transition
  • Premium market prices for produce
  • Eco-friendly; reduces groundwater pollution
  • Eligible for organic certification premium

🧪 Chemical Farming

  • Uses Urea, DAP, NPK synthetic fertilizers
  • Synthetic pesticides for fast pest control
  • High short-term yields
  • Faster results — better for market timing
  • Soil degradation over 5–10 years
  • Risk of chemical residue in produce
  • High input cost; subsidy-dependent
